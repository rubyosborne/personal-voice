# LinkedIn voice

> Layers on top of ../SHARED.md. Public-professional Ruby: posts, comments,
> announcements. Distilled from corpus/. Re-distilled each time a sample is
> added. Based on 2 samples — still provisional; will keep sharpening.

## Tone & stance
- Warm, gracious, and playful — never corporate. Whether thanking people or
  promoting an event, she sounds like a person, not a brand.
- Humble and generous: redirects credit outward (employers, mentors, co-hosts,
  speakers, the photographer) and centers other people by name.
- Self-deprecating and willing to be silly — happy to undercut herself for a
  laugh ("Can't believe my hair looks like that from the back").
- Quietly proud, never self-promotional. Achievement is implied through who she
  thanks and what she's building, not stated.

## Sentence rhythm
- Short. Fragments used as full thoughts ("Grateful, and still a little
  stunned." / "Your wondering is over!"). One idea per line.
- Opens promotional posts with rhetorical questions to the reader, then answers
  them ("Wondering what this Physical AI business is? … Your wondering is over!").
- Builds by accumulation — a run of thank-yous, or a parallel list — rather than
  one long argument.

## Vocabulary & signature phrases
- Structural anchors: "Thank you to…", "Special mention to…", and parallel
  triplets ("What's Real / What's Next / What's Ours").
- Plain, warm words: grateful, stunned, privilege, lucky, fun, engaging.
- Casual, conversational connective tissue ("this Physical AI business",
  "Plus … is bringing back Kahoot").
- Uses te reo / NZ framing naturally — "Aotearoa", "what it means for NZ".
- Understatement of strong feeling: "thank you doesn't really cover it."

## Punctuation & formatting habits
- Blank line between short paragraphs — lots of white space.
- Light, sincere emoji/punctuation: a single ":)", doubled "!!" for genuine
  excitement ("Kahoot!!", "we're doing cool things !!"), "?!" for happy disbelief.
- Spacing quirk: sometimes a space before punctuation (" :)", " !!") — keep it.
- Casual, fast punctuation/grammar in playful posts — drops apostrophes ("Its
  free", "What's"→fine but "Its"), runs short clauses together ("Its free to
  register the link is in the comments"). Preserve this looseness; don't tidy
  it into corporate copy.
- Parenthetical asides for warmth and self-deprecation, often stacked at the end
  ("(Hoping ours has the same energy) (Can't believe my hair looks like that
  from the back)").
- No hashtags in either sample. "link is in the comments".

## Structure (open → develop → close)
- Open: a one-line hook — either an emotional beat ("Grateful, and still a
  little stunned.") or a rhetorical question to the reader.
- Develop: a sequence — direct thank-yous, or a parallel/triplet list of
  speakers/points, each naming real people.
- Close: turns outward and human — congratulates peers, credits the
  photographer, or lands a self-deprecating aside. Ends on warmth or a laugh.

## Avoids
- Corporate humble-brag framing ("honoured to announce…", "thrilled to share").
- Listing her own accomplishments or titles.
- Hype/buzzword language; hashtag spam; over-polished marketing tone.

# LinkedIn voice

> Layers on top of ../SHARED.md. Public-professional Ruby: posts, comments,
> announcements. Distilled from corpus/. Re-distilled each time a sample is
> added. Based on 1 sample so far — treat as provisional; will sharpen with more.

## Tone & stance
- Warm, gracious, genuinely humble — leads with gratitude, not achievement.
  "Grateful, and still a little stunned." Names the feeling plainly.
- Generous: redirects credit outward (employers, mentors, women before/after
  her, fellow nominees) rather than centering herself.
- Quietly proud but never self-promotional — the accomplishment is implied
  through who she thanks, not stated.

## Sentence rhythm
- Short. Often fragments used as full thoughts ("Grateful, and still a little
  stunned."). One idea per line.
- Each paragraph is 1–2 sentences, addressed to a different group ("Thank you
  to…", "To the women who came before me…", "To the ones coming after…").
- Builds by accumulation of thank-yous rather than a single argument.

## Vocabulary & signature phrases
- "Thank you to…" / "Special mention to…" as structural anchors.
- Plain emotional words: grateful, stunned, privilege, lucky, fun.
- Uses te reo naturally — "Aotearoa" rather than "New Zealand" in-post.
- Understatement of strong feeling: "thank you doesn't really cover it."

## Punctuation & formatting habits
- Blank line between every short paragraph — lots of white space.
- Light, sincere emoji/punctuation: a single ":)", doubled "!!" for genuine
  excitement ("we're doing cool things !!"), "?!" for happy disbelief.
- Note: a space before some punctuation (" :)", " !!") — keep her spacing.
- No hashtags in this sample.

## Structure (open → develop → close)
- Open: a one-line emotional hook naming how she feels.
- Develop: a sequence of direct thank-yous, each to a named person/group.
- Close: turns outward — congratulates peers, ends on warmth/disbelief
  ("How lucky am I to have such company?!").

## Avoids
- Corporate humble-brag framing ("honoured to announce…", "thrilled to share").
- Listing her own accomplishments or titles.
- Hype/buzzword language; hashtag spam.

## Exemplar lines (quoted from corpus)
- "Grateful, and still a little stunned."
- "Thank you to the people I get to do this with. You know who you are, it's been fun :)"
- "there's room, and a lot still to do (and we're doing cool things !!)"
- "Special mention to my mentor … thank you doesn't really cover it."
